## Quick Answer

**Federal income tax is a tax levied by the U.S. government on your earnings, using a progressive system of seven brackets ranging from 10% to 37%.** It is the largest tax most workers pay and is collected through [paycheck withholding](/learn/what-is-tax-withholding/) throughout the year. Your actual tax bill depends on your [filing status](/learn/filing-status-explained/), deductions, and credits — not just your salary.

## How Federal Income Tax Is Calculated

Your federal income tax follows a specific calculation path:

### Step 1: Determine Gross Income
Add up all income sources — salary, [bonuses](/learn/how-bonuses-are-taxed/), [side hustle income](/learn/taxes-on-side-hustle-income/), investment gains, and other earnings. This is your [gross income](/learn/what-is-gross-pay/).

### Step 2: Calculate AGI
Subtract above-the-line deductions like [401(k) contributions](/learn/how-401k-reduces-taxes/), [HSA contributions](/learn/what-is-an-hsa/), and student loan interest to get your [Adjusted Gross Income (AGI)](/learn/what-is-agi/).

### Step 3: Apply Deductions
Subtract either the [standard deduction](/learn/what-is-standard-deduction/) ($15,000 single / $30,000 married in 2025) or your itemized deductions, whichever is greater. This gives you your [taxable income](/learn/what-is-taxable-income/).

### Step 4: Apply Tax Brackets
Your taxable income is taxed through the [progressive brackets](/learn/what-are-tax-brackets/). Each portion of income is taxed at its bracket rate.

### Step 5: Subtract Credits
Tax credits reduce your tax bill dollar-for-dollar. Common credits include the Child Tax Credit ($2,000 per qualifying child) and education credits.

Marcus is single, earns $90,000, contributes $8,000 to his 401(k), and lives in [Texas](/texas/). No dependents.

Federal tax on $67,000:
- 10% on $11,925 = $1,192.50
- 12% on $36,550 = $4,386.00
- 22% on $18,525 = $4,075.50

## Federal Income Tax vs Other Taxes

Federal income tax is just one piece of the puzzle. Your paycheck also gets hit with:

- **[FICA taxes](/learn/what-is-fica/)**: Social Security (6.2%) plus Medicare (1.45%) = 7.65% on every dollar
- **State income tax**: Ranges from 0% in [Florida](/florida/) and [Texas](/texas/) to over 13% in [California](/california/). See [how state taxes work](/learn/how-state-taxes-work/)
- **[Local income taxes](/learn/local-income-taxes/)**: Some cities like New York City add additional tax

For someone in the 22% federal bracket living in a state with 5% tax, the combined marginal rate on the next dollar earned is roughly 34.65% (22% + 5% + 7.65% FICA).

## Filing and Payment

Most employees pay federal income tax through [withholding](/learn/what-is-tax-withholding/) — money taken from each paycheck. [Self-employed](/learn/what-is-self-employment-tax/) workers must make [quarterly estimated payments](/learn/quarterly-estimated-taxes/) instead.

Your annual tax return (due April 15) reconciles what was withheld versus what you actually owe. Compare your situation across filing statuses with the [married calculator](/married/) or estimate your take-home pay in any state with the [SalaryHog calculator](/).
