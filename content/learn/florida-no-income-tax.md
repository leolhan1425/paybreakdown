## Quick Answer

**Florida has no state income tax on individuals, meaning your paycheck is only reduced by [federal taxes](/learn/federal-income-tax-explained/) and [FICA](/learn/what-is-fica/).** Combined with moderate property taxes (average 0.8%) and a 6% sales tax, Florida offers one of the lowest overall tax burdens in the country. It is the most popular destination for people relocating from [high-tax states](/learn/highest-tax-states/) like New York and California.

## How Much You Save in Florida

Compared to high-tax states, the income tax savings in Florida are substantial:

| Income | Savings vs California | Savings vs New York | Savings vs New Jersey |
|--------|----------------------|--------------------|----------------------|
| $60,000 | ~$2,100 | ~$2,800 | ~$2,000 |
| $85,000 | ~$3,600 | ~$4,100 | ~$3,500 |
| $120,000 | ~$6,300 | ~$6,200 | ~$5,200 |
| $200,000 | ~$13,800 | ~$12,000 | ~$10,500 |

At $120,000, moving from New York to Florida saves over $500/month in state income tax alone. See your specific numbers with the [relocation tool](/relocate/).

## Florida vs Texas: The Two Most Popular No-Tax States

| Factor | Florida | [Texas](/texas/) |
|--------|---------|--------|
| Income tax | 0% | 0% |
| Sales tax (state) | 6.0% | 6.25% |
| Sales tax (max with local) | 7.5% | 8.25% |
| Avg property tax | 0.80% | 1.60% |
| Estate tax | None | None |
| Corporate income tax | 5.5% | None |
| Homestead exemption | Up to $50,000 | Yes, but smaller |

For homeowners, Florida is often the better deal. A $400,000 home in Florida costs about $3,200/year in property tax vs $6,400 in Texas. That $3,200 difference can offset many other cost-of-living factors.

## What Florida Residents Pay Instead

### Sales Tax
Florida's 6% state sales tax (plus up to 1.5% local surtax) applies to most purchases. Groceries are exempt. On $35,000 in annual taxable spending, that is about $2,450 in sales tax.

### Property Tax
Florida's average effective rate is 0.80%, but this varies by county:

| County | Effective Rate | Tax on $400K Home |
|--------|---------------|-------------------|
| Miami-Dade | 0.86% | $3,440 |
| Broward (Ft. Lauderdale) | 0.93% | $3,720 |
| Orange (Orlando) | 0.85% | $3,400 |
| Hillsborough (Tampa) | 0.92% | $3,680 |
| Duval (Jacksonville) | 0.82% | $3,280 |

Florida's generous homestead exemption (up to $50,000 off assessed value) and Save Our Homes cap (3% annual increase limit) help keep property taxes low for long-term residents.

### Insurance
Florida homeowners face high insurance costs, especially in hurricane-prone areas. This is a significant cost that partially offsets tax savings.

## Who Benefits Most from Florida

- **High earners**: No ceiling on income tax savings
- **Retirees**: No tax on Social Security, pensions, 401(k) distributions, or IRA withdrawals. See [taxes in retirement](/learn/taxes-when-you-retire/)
- **Homeowners**: Low property tax rate with strong homestead protections
- **Remote workers**: Keep a high salary from a [high-tax state employer](/learn/working-remotely-across-state-lines/) while paying zero state tax
- **Business owners**: No personal income tax on [self-employment income](/learn/what-is-self-employment-tax/)
