## Quick Answer

**Texas charges no state income tax, which means your paycheck is only reduced by [federal income tax](/learn/federal-income-tax-explained/) and [FICA](/learn/what-is-fica/) — no state tax line on your [pay stub](/learn/how-to-read-your-pay-stub/).** This can save you thousands per year compared to states like [California](/california/) or [New York](/new-york/). However, Texas compensates with higher sales taxes (6.25% state + up to 2% local = 8.25% total) and some of the highest property taxes in the country (averaging 1.6%).

## How Much You Save in Texas

Here is the state income tax you avoid by living in Texas instead of other states:

| Income | CA Tax | NY Tax | TX Tax | Savings vs CA | Savings vs NY |
|--------|--------|--------|--------|---------------|---------------|
| $50,000 | ~$1,600 | ~$2,400 | $0 | $1,600 | $2,400 |
| $75,000 | ~$3,000 | ~$3,800 | $0 | $3,000 | $3,800 |
| $100,000 | ~$5,000 | ~$5,200 | $0 | $5,000 | $5,200 |
| $150,000 | ~$9,500 | ~$8,500 | $0 | $9,500 | $8,500 |

At $100,000, living in Texas saves you over $400/month compared to California. See your exact savings at the [relocation tool](/relocate/).

## Real Example With Actual Numbers

James earns $85,000 in Austin, Texas. He is single with no dependents.

| Deduction | Annual | Per Biweekly Paycheck |
|-----------|--------|-----------------------|
| Federal income tax | ~$10,300 | ~$396 |
| Social Security (6.2%) | $5,270 | $202.69 |
| Medicare (1.45%) | $1,232.50 | $47.40 |
| State income tax | $0 | $0 |
| **Total tax** | **$16,802.50** | **$646.09** |
| **Take-home pay** | **$68,197.50** | **$2,623.14** |

James keeps about 80.2% of his gross pay. In California, he would keep about 74.5%. In New York City, about 71.2%. Try the [SalaryHog calculator](/) to compare.

## What Texans Pay Instead

### Sales Tax
The combined state and local sales tax in Texas can reach 8.25%. If you spend $30,000/year on taxable goods and services, that is about $2,475 in sales tax. Groceries, medicine, and certain essentials are exempt.

### Property Tax
Texas property taxes average 1.6% but can exceed 2% in some counties:

| Metro Area | Avg Property Tax Rate | Tax on $350K Home |
|------------|----------------------|-------------------|
| Houston | 1.8% | $6,300 |
| Dallas | 1.9% | $6,650 |
| Austin | 1.6% | $5,600 |
| San Antonio | 1.7% | $5,950 |

For homeowners, property tax can partially or fully offset the income tax savings. Renters benefit most from Texas's no-income-tax policy because property tax is less directly felt.

## Texas vs Other No-Tax States

| State | Sales Tax | Avg Property Tax | Other |
|-------|-----------|-----------------|-------|
| Texas | 6.25% (+ local) | 1.6% | High property tax |
| [Florida](/florida/) | 6.0% (+ local) | 0.8% | Lower property tax |
| Nevada | 6.85% (+ local) | 0.53% | Very low property tax |
| Wyoming | 4.0% | 0.55% | Lowest overall |
| Washington | 6.5% (+ local) | 0.87% | Has capital gains tax |

For homeowners, Florida and Nevada may offer a lower total tax burden than Texas because of property tax differences. For renters and high earners, Texas is very competitive.

## Who Benefits Most from Texas

- **High-income earners**: The more you earn, the more you save in state income tax
- **Renters**: You avoid high property tax directly
- **Remote workers**: Keep a high out-of-state salary while paying no state tax. See [remote work tax rules](/learn/working-remotely-across-state-lines/)
- **Self-employed workers**: No state income tax on [self-employment earnings](/learn/what-is-self-employment-tax/) either. Try the [freelance calculator](/freelance/)
