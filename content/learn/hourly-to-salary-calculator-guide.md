## Quick Answer

**To convert an hourly wage to an annual salary, multiply your hourly rate by 2,080 (40 hours per week times 52 weeks).** For example, $30/hour equals $62,400 per year. This standard formula assumes full-time work with no unpaid time off. Your actual [take-home pay](/learn/what-is-net-pay/) will be lower after taxes and deductions.

## The Basic Formula

**Hourly Rate x 2,080 = Annual Salary**

| Hourly Rate | Annual Salary |
|-------------|--------------|
| $15/hour | $31,200 |
| $20/hour | $41,600 |
| $25/hour | $52,000 |
| $30/hour | $62,400 |
| $40/hour | $83,200 |
| $50/hour | $104,000 |
| $75/hour | $156,000 |

For other time periods:
- **Weekly**: Hourly rate x 40
- **Biweekly**: Hourly rate x 80
- **Monthly**: Hourly rate x 173.33
- **Annually**: Hourly rate x 2,080

## Real Example With Actual Numbers

Taylor earns $28/hour in [Texas](/texas/) and works 40 hours per week.

**Gross annual salary**: $28 x 2,080 = **$58,240**

Now let's calculate [take-home pay](/learn/what-is-net-pay/):

| Deduction | Annual | Per Biweekly Paycheck |
|-----------|--------|-----------------------|
| Gross pay | $58,240 | $2,240 |
| Federal income tax | -$5,350 | -$205.77 |
| Social Security (6.2%) | -$3,610.88 | -$138.88 |
| Medicare (1.45%) | -$844.48 | -$32.48 |
| **Take-home** | **$48,434.64** | **$1,862.87** |

Taylor's $28/hour translates to about $1,863 every two weeks after federal taxes. No state tax because Texas has [no income tax](/learn/texas-no-income-tax/). Use the [SalaryHog calculator](/) to see the full breakdown for your hourly rate and state.

## Adjusting for Overtime

If you regularly work [overtime](/learn/how-overtime-is-taxed/), your annual earnings will be higher. Overtime is paid at 1.5x your regular rate for [non-exempt employees](/learn/exempt-vs-nonexempt/).

Taylor works 5 overtime hours per week:
- Regular: 40 hours x $28 = $1,120/week
- Overtime: 5 hours x $42 (1.5x) = $210/week
- Weekly total: $1,330
- **Annual with OT: $69,160** (vs $58,240 without OT)

## Adjusting for Unpaid Time Off

If you are hourly and do not receive paid time off, reduce the weeks accordingly:

| Unpaid Weeks Off | Working Weeks | Annual Salary ($28/hr) |
|-----------------|---------------|----------------------|
| 0 | 52 | $58,240 |
| 1 | 51 | $57,120 |
| 2 | 50 | $56,000 |
| 3 | 49 | $54,880 |
| 4 | 48 | $53,760 |

Two weeks of unpaid vacation reduces annual earnings by $2,240.

## Comparing Hourly vs Salaried Positions

When evaluating a job offer, do not just compare the gross numbers. A salaried position at $55,000 may be worth more than $28/hour ($58,240) because salaried jobs typically include:

- Paid time off (2-4 weeks)
- Employer-sponsored health insurance
- [401(k) match](/learn/employer-match-explained/)
- Paid holidays
- Overtime protection (or lack thereof — see [exempt vs non-exempt](/learn/exempt-vs-nonexempt/))

See [salary vs total compensation](/learn/salary-vs-total-compensation/) for a comprehensive comparison.
